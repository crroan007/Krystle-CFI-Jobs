## Notes

- **Wet rate** = fuel included. **Dry rate** = add $40-50/hr for fuel.
- Atlanta Flying Club rates are DRY ($100-130/hr + fuel).
- West GA at $75/hr is by far the cheapest but is a 45 min drive.
- For time-building, the C152 at West GA is the most cost-effective option.
- Club memberships add $1,000-$2,000/year in fixed costs -- factor this into total cost.

## Cheapest Path (Rental Only)

If Krystle had to rent all 300 hours:
- **Best case**: 300 hrs x $75/hr at West GA = **$22,500**
- **Mid case**: 300 hrs x $140/hr at Atlanta Flying Club = **$42,000** + $350 initiation + $1,050 dues = **$43,400**
- **Worst case**: 300 hrs x $280/hr at Aero Atlanta = **$84,000** + fees = **$88,000+**

## Recommendation

**Do not rent unless absolutely necessary.** Getting hired as CFII is 10x more efficient:
- Earn $25-60/hr instead of paying $75-280/hr
- Same flight hours logged either way
- Net difference per hour: $100-340 (what you'd pay vs what you'd earn)
